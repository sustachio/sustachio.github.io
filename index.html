Berlin Wall

How to use this site
What was the Berlin wall?
The Berlin wall was a wall that separated the two sides of berlin.
It was built in 1961 and taken down in 1989.

It started as a barbed wire fence, but was later rebuilt with concrete blocks that were 12 feet high and four feet wide.
Why was it built?
Before the Berlin Wall was built, around 2.5 million East Germans had fled to West Germany. This was not good for the Soviet Union, because they were losing a lot of workers.

In response to all of the people leaving they built the Berlin wall. The wall was very hard to pass because it was guarded by a lot of soldiers. The wall made sure that barley anyone could escape Soviet-controlled East Berlin.
